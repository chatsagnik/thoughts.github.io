Day 1: Arrive late at the campus after Indigo screws up (foreshadowing things to come). Meet Jatin and Santanu at approximately 4pm. Normal hi hello meet and greet right? Nope. We plunge into a 1.5 hour long session on robust statistics. TIFR canteen - restaurants would kill to have this view. Return home, order groceries.

Day 2: Medical + Administrative hoopla + meet various non-teaching staff in the department to get setup. Everyone very warm and accomodating. First talk at 4 - Gaurav Sinha from Microsoft on GLMs - Zoom did half the talking. Talk not recorded but excellent discussions. Kavitha introduces me to many of the department. Kavitha is an excellent warm individual. I return home and make mixed veg.

Day 3: Workstation (imac) + Wifi + Office gets straightened out. Try to work for a bit. Meet Jatin for a quick chat - turns into a small discussion. Meet Arkadev. Finish all administrative tasks - got my id! Ian Mertz gives a talk at the TIFR colloquium on catalytic computation. Excellent ideas: Compression is lack of randomness. I make chicken curry!

Day 4: I start working finally! Talk by Sharat Ibrahimpur - Piyush has a doubt (balls and bins but with ghost balls O(log log n/log log log n)!!!) and it goes into full blown discussion mode - Raghuvansh Saxena, Ramprasad, Rahul Vaze and Sharat trying to convince Piyush that the ideas work out.

Day 5: Leonard Brice talk in the morning. Another intense round of discussions started by Arkadev. Maggi and eggs.

Day 6: Mix veg. Day of meetings with Jatin and Shantanu.
